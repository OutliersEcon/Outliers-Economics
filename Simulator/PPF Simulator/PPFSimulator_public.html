About the Simulator

Cobb-Douglas Utility Function: Represents consumer preferences:

U(X, Y) = Xᵅ * Y⁽¹⁻ᵅ⁾

Production Possibility Frontier (PPF): Shows feasible combinations of Good X and Good Y given resource constraints.

Straight Line PPF: Constant opportunity cost:

aX + bY = R

Concave PPF: Increasing opportunity cost (simplified):

X² + Y² = R

Utility Maximization: The optimal point occurs where the indifference curve is tangent to the PPF, satisfying:

MRS = MRT, where MRS = (α/(1-α)) * (Y/X)

Inputs:

  • Total Resources (R): Total resource constraint.
  • Tech Parameter X (a): Resources required per unit of Good X.
  • Tech Parameter Y (b): Resources required per unit of Good Y.
  • Alpha (α): Preference for Good X in the utility function.
